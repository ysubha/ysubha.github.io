How I think about testing

Three principles I actually work by

01 · Failure patterns > test counts

A suite of 2,000 tests is only as good as the failure modes it was designed around. I start from audit logs and production data - the ten ways something goes quietly wrong - and work backwards to test cases.

02 · The cheapest bug is the one never built

My best catch wasn't automated at all: a design flaw spotted at 75% build, on domain knowledge. Automation scales judgment - it doesn't replace it. Shift-left means being in the design review, not just the pipeline.

03 · A test I can't defend is a test I delete

Every assertion should answer "what production failure does this prevent?" If it can't, it's maintenance debt wearing a green checkmark. Same rule I apply to my resume: every claim must survive drilling.
